# RDF Navigator product profile

## Register

navigator

## Users

RDF and ontology authors, standards editors, web developers, and agent-tool
builders inspecting semantic information in an HTML document. They are usually
debugging or reviewing a live document and need to move quickly between RDF
statements, their DOM carriers, and vocabulary definitions.

## Product Purpose

Make the RDF dataset expressed by IA² markup immediately legible and
navigable. Success means a user can understand the current live semantic state,
trace a rendered statement back to its visible carrier when one exists, and
open authoritative vocabulary definitions without leaving the inspection flow.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, trustworthy.

## Anti-references

- Opaque developer inspectors that expose syntax without provenance or links.
- Decorative dashboards that turn document inspection into visual noise.
- Modal-first interfaces that obscure the document being inspected.
- Controls that remain visible when their action cannot produce a useful result.

## Design Principles

1. Semantics before serialization: lead with the navigable statement model.
2. Show an affordance only when it can complete its promise.
3. Keep linked data linked: vocabulary IRIs should lead to their definitions.
4. Reflect live state clearly while preserving explicit refresh and diagnostics.
5. Prefer familiar, keyboard-accessible product controls over novel interaction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA behavior. Preserve keyboard operation, visible focus,
reduced-motion support, sufficient contrast in both color schemes, descriptive
control names, and layouts that remain usable at narrow viewport widths.
