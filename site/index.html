Information Architecture for Intelligent Agents

The web should be legible to agents without becoming theirs.

That means more than making pages easy to parse. It means preserving what makes the web powerful: independently published resources, explicit structure, rich relationships, and links that cross domains. IA² lets people, applications, and agents exchange that meaning openly, while keeping it inspectable in the same surface a person reads.

Read the HTML/RDF model ↗ Try the live demo

HTML is the first proving ground. An open, interconnected information commons is the larger horizon.

01 / Exchange

Share meaning, not just text.

Markdown gives agents a readable common denominator. But as an interchange it usually turns entities, relationships, evidence, constraints, and provenance back into prose for every receiver to interpret again.

IA² keeps the explanation and its information graph together. One agent can publish a claim, plan, decision, result, or live state; another can follow its exact identities, relationships, evidence, and context across independently published surfaces while a person inspects the same information.

02 / Architectural horizon

A legibility layer for more than pages.

Each layer answers a different question. Keeping them separate is what makes the whole architecture composable.

  1. 01

    Surfaces and bindings

    Where does the information live, and how does it relate to what a person sees?

    HTML and live DOM today; more host-native bindings later.
  2. 02

    Semantic models

    What does the information mean?

    Full RDF 1.2, open vocabularies, profiles, and mappings.
  3. 03

    Integrity and evidence

    Why should a consumer accept, qualify, or revisit it?

    SHACL, provenance, confidence, validation, and signatures.
  4. 04

    Agency

    What could be done, under which conditions, and by whose authority?

    Inspectable affordances with execution kept separate.
  5. 05

    Navigation and exchange

    How does information move without losing identity or context?

    Discovery, fragments, snapshots, queries, diffs, and subscriptions.

04 / North star

Independently published information should remain rich, linked, and intelligible to people, applications, and agents.

The result is not one universal agent API. Each surface can explain its own identities, relationships, evidence, and constraints, then link outward on open terms. Agents can participate in that commons without first translating it into a platform-owned schema.
